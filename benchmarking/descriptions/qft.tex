The Quantum Fourier Transform (QFT) function is a quantum algorithmic primitive, utilized in algorithms such as Shor's Factoring, Discrete Logarithm and Quantum Phase Estimation. It is the quantum analog of the discrete Fourier transform, applied to a quantum state vector.
We benchmark the QFT on \(n\) qubits by preparing the equal superposition (comb) state
\[
|\psi_0\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2^n}} \sum_{t=0}^{2^n-1} |t\rangle = H^{\otimes n}|0^n\rangle ,
\]
applying the QFT, and comparing the measured output distribution to the expected outcome \(|\psi_f\rangle = |0^n\rangle\).
The score is defined from the total variation distance between the observed output distribution \(p\) and the ideal distribution \(u\),
\[
D_{\mathrm{TV}}(p,u) = \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i=0}^{2^n-1} |p_i - u_i| .
\]
We then define the score:
\[
S = 1 - \frac{D_{\mathrm{TV}}(p,u)}{1 - 1/n} .
\]
(This example can be run with different comb density of size $m$: $|\psi_0 \rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2^{N-m}}}\sum_{t=0}^{2^{N-m}-1}|t 2^m\rangle.)$
